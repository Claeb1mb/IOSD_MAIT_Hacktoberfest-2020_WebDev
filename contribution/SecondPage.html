  • Constituents

    Tobacco is a green, leafy plant that contains a chemical called nicotine. It is dried, ground up and used in many different ways. It can be chewed or sniffed. It supports a billion dollar industry.

    When tobacco burns, thousands of chemical substances including 40 known cancer causing substances like carbon monoxide and nitric oxide as well as trace amount of cancer causing radioactive particles are released.

  • Effluents

    Tobacco upon burning releases several gases. Theses are again harmful to us. Some amog those are Carbon Monoxide, Nitric Oxide, Hydrogen Cyanide, Ammonia e.t.c.

    A recent study states that when we consume tobacco in any form, only 30% of the content exits our body. The rest 70% is absorbed by differnt organs of our body leading to many health risks.
